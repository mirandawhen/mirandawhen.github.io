Animal Cop

Animal Cop

January - May 2017

BACKGROUND

Animal shelters provide crucial services to promote animal welfare that include the prosecution and prevention of animal abuse or neglect. However, investigations are complicated.

  • Concerned, well-meaning citizens get confused, often contact the wrong authority, too late and without the necessary evidence
  • Staff officers are overwhelmed without this evidence and can't prosecute animal cases the necessary action taking action and successfully prosecuting criminal cases.

We worked with the Humane Animal Rescue shelter to understand how technology could better support animal welfare through a service design framework.



EXPLORATORY RESEARCH

We used a variety of methonds including a literature review, competitive analysis, and interview to understand the entire problem space. By the end of this process we became experts in animal laws and humane investigations.

Primary research goals and interview groups.

SYNTHESIZING INSIGHTS

Based on our research, we conducted affinity diagramming and create models to communicate our insights. We discovered 5 main insights.

1. The main goal of animal rescue is to educate, not confiscate

We modeled the typical workflow of a humane officer as they investigate cases.

A humane officer’s primary role is to educate by giving people the resources necessary to care for their animals. Interviewed highlighted that officers give as many resources to the animal owner as possible, rather than immediately taking the animal.

2. Laws are complicated and don't match people's assumptions

Citizens don’t know what legally constitutes animal abuse, sometimes reporting cases that aren't abuse. There are multiple layers of laws and current resources are difficult for citizens to find and comprehend.

3. Misreporting can have serious consequences for the organization, people, and animals involved

Reporters often provide incomplete information and even false information. This leads to serious drains on the already limited resources of the humane officer.

5. For citizen reporters, lack of feedback after reporting is frustrating and disheartening.

Currently, citizens get no feedback about cases. Without this feedback, citizens become concerned that nothing is being done and question whether their actions have actually helped the situation.

Mapping out key stakeholders and their relationship.

All of our insights suggest that

  • We should reduce the work on the humane officer.
  • Make it easier for citizens to provide the correct information
  • Educate citizens about actual animal laws

IDEATION

Co-creating imagined futures

Working off our our insights, we hosted a participatory design session with our clients. This was meant to better understand their goals and to generate a wide variety of ideas.

Testing our hypothesis by speed dating

After coming up with a variety of scenarios, we tested our concepts through speed dating sessions with a variety of potential users. Based on this fedback, we were able to prioritize key features for our solutions.

DESIGN DECISIONS

Reduce misappropriate reporting

Highlight the most common misconceptions in a way that captures people's attention. We can't expect everyone to read or to learn, so we had to place things in a way that had the most impact.



Educate reporters about animal laws and humane officer roles

Misinformation impacts the way that users send in their reports and the information that they put it.



Bring accountability into the system

The major feedback from our work was that reporters don't get the information that they truly need.


DESIGN OVERVIEW

As a student that takes classes in creative disciplines, this project was very useful for myself. It helped me understand ways to better work in creative groups. Some lessons I learned include the following:

  • Defining success in academic settings is difficult and contingent on your pedagogical framework
  • Build upon current work to supplement existing bodies of knowledge
  • Visual design is a crucial part of the overall experience, especially when the function of the app is heavily dependent on the information hierarchy and legibility of the content.
