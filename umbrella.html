Cisco Umbrella: Re-Imagining the management and creation of security policies.

Overview

During the Summer of 2016, I interned as a UX Designer and Researcher for OpenDNS, a part Cisco. OpenDNS is a DNS resolution and cybersecurity company that was acquired by Cisco in 2015. During my summer, I worked on Cisco Umbrella - a new SaaS enterprise security product. I spent my time designing a new user flow for cybersecurity and IT professionals that manage security policies. I also leveraged research methods such as customer calls, usage data, and remote testing to validate and informs designs.

My Role: Product Design, User Research

Team: OpenDNS UX Group

Context: Internship, Summer 2016

Process


Design

Due to an NDA, I can't reveal too much about my design work. However, I worked with my team to re-design the process of creating and managing security policies. We focused on creating a process that adapts to the complexity of a user's security needs. For high power users, we needed to ensure that they had the support to implement the most powerful security features of Umbrella. In coming up with designs, I had the opportunity to create a new interaction pattern that will be incorporated into the final Umbrella design.


Research

Evaluating Solutions through Remote Testing

I designed and deployed 8 different remote usability studies. This work was used to evaluate a new table pattern, a new data visualization, a navigation interaction, website IA, button placement, and other design patterns. During my research process, I utilized techniques such as task-analysis, A/B testing, card sorting, and tree testing. I also incorporated survey questions from the System Usability Scale into these studies to gain further insight.


Informing Decisions with Usage Data

I utilized usage data to understand how users were currently using the product. I guaged how important certain configurations were based on how many people were actually using them. With this insight, I decided whether or not certain features from the original OpenDNS Umbrella product should be implemented in Cisco Umbrella.


Identifying Opportunities from Customer Calls

I worked with project managers and customer success representatives to conduct customer calls. Because the UX team was handling a massive overhaul of the product, we wanted to ensure that customers and users would be involved in the design process. In conducting customer calls and sending customer surveys, we identified certain needs like increased customizability and flexibility for policy creation. We also shared designs from the new policy creation process before moving forward with the development process.
