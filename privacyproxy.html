Privacy Proxy: Empowering people to protect their personal information on smartphone applications.

Overview

During the Fall of 2016, I worked as a UX Designer for Privacy Proxy - an android application that uses crowdsourced data to detect and notify users of personally identifiable information (PII) leaks. I conducted heuristic analysis to propose small scale re-designs and planned remote user testing sessions. I also had the opportunity to dabble in logo design and Android UI development.

My Role: Interaction Designer, UX Researcher, Logo Design

Team Members: Gaurav Srivastava, Saksham Chitkara

Context: Research Project, Fall 2016

Lab: CMU Computer Human Interaction: Mobile Privacy Security

Problem Space

"Over 1.4 billion smartphones were sold worldwide in 2015. The growing user base translates into increasing incentive for companies and individuals to enter the mobile app market. However, the wealth of personal information that an app can access through a smartphone poses a serious privacy concern. In particular, personally identifiable information (PII), which is “information that can be used on its own or with other information to identify, contact, or locate a single person, or to identify an individual in context”, should be treated with the utmost caution."

Kevin Ku

Problem

Many users do not realize that smartphone applications can access and collect PII.

Design

I conducted heuristic evaluations and offered solutions to re-design aspects concerning data visualization, micro UI interactions, and labelling. In addition to addressing heuristic violations, the purpose of these re-designs was to considers ways to promote sensemaking. PrivacyProxy is supposed to empower users by presenting actionable data.

With this purpose in mind, I redesigned the representation of application summary information. I added visual heirarchy and moved a data visualization from the within app screen into the summary app page. Screen size was a major constrait. In moving this visualization, I recommended a bar chart representation to reduce screen size and to follow good visualization techniques. These design change made information more visible to users and allows users to compare this information between applications. In representing information in such a manner, this information can be actionable for users.
