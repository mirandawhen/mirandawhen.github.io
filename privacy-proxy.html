Privacy Proxy

Privacy Proxy

August - December 2017

BACKGROUND

PrivacyProxy is an android application that uses crowdsourced data to detect and notify users of personally identifiable information (PII) leaks. I re-designed the application to include better visualizations and more effectively communicate its uses to users. I was the only designer on this project and worked closely with engineers for implementation.





PAST WORK

Phone Applications are a privacy concern.

The wealth of personal information that an app can access through a smartphone poses a serious privacy concern. The goal of this work was to use crowdsourcing to understand the behavior of their applications by answering

  • Which applications send out information?
  • Where is information sent? :
  • What information is being sent?

USER RESEARCH

Talking to experts.

DESIGN DECISIONS

Create a dashboard that leads to sensemaking and call to actions.

The previous iteration had a very meaningless dashboard that merely showed data rather than information.

Make it easier to crowdsource

The goal of this application is meant to gather and disseminate crowd-sourced data. Thus, we needed incentives for users to actually input data.

Experts are lazy, newbies are overwhelmed.

Provide smart summaries and filtering of the information present. This will make it easier to read through and manage.
