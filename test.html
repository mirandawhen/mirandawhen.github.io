Privacy Proxy: Empowering people to protect their personal information on smartphone applications.

Overview

During the Fall of 2016, I worked as a UX Designer for Privacy Proxy - an android application that uses crowdsourced data to detect and notify users of personally identifiable information (PII) leaks. I conducted heuristic analysis to propose small scale re-designs and planned remote user testing sessions. I also had the opportunity to dabble in logo design and Android UI development.

Problem Space

"Over 1.4 billion smartphones were sold worldwide in 2015. The growing user base translates into increasing incentive for companies and individuals to enter the mobile app market. However, the wealth of personal information that an app can access through a smartphone poses a serious privacy concern. In particular, personally identifiable information (PII), which is “information that can be used on its own or with other information to identify, contact, or locate a single person, or to identify an individual in context”, should be treated with the utmost caution."

Kevin Ku

Problem

Many users do not realize that smartphone applications can access and collect PII.

Solution

Privacy Proxy: An application that that informs and empowers users.
