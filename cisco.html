Cisco Umbrella

Creating a security solution that is simple to manage and easy to deploy.

During the Summer of 2016, I interned as a UX Designer and Researcher for OpenDNS, a part Cisco. OpenDNS is a DNS resolution and cybersecurity company that was acquired by Cisco in 2015. During my summer, I worked on Cisco Umbrella - a new SaaS enterprise security product.

I spent the summer designing a new user flow for cybersecurity and IT professionals that managing security policies. I also leveraged user research techniques such as customer calls, usage data, and remote testing to evaluate designs.


Design

In doing so, I referred to principles from the Material and Lightning design systems. Furthermore, I used tools such as Sketch and InVision actually create my designs.

Research

Without a dedicated UX Researcher on the team, I had the opportunity to conduct a variety of research projects in collaboration with members from Product Management, Web Design, and Customer Succces.


Identifying Design Opportunities from Customer Calls

I worked with project managers and customer success representatives to conduct customer calls. These calls were used to identify user needs and to construct user scenarios.


Evaluating Design Solutions through Remote Testing

I designed and deployed 8 different remote usability studies. This work was conducted with the tool usertesting.com. I used a variety of techniques including task-analysis, A/B testing, card sorting, and tree testing. I also incorporated survey questions from the System Usability Scale into these studies to gain further insight.


Informing Design Decisions with Usage Data

I utilized usage data to determine whether or not certain features from the original OpenDNS Umbrella product would be implementing in Cisco Umbrella.


Reflections

This was a wonderful summer where I got to apply my design and research skills for a truly interesting product. Working within an industry environment was challenging because I was designing for an enterprise product and conducting research under industry constraints. However, it was a fun challenge to tackle over the summer.
